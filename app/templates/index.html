About Me

I'm a senior at Vassar College double-majoring in Computer Science and Astronomy, based in Queens, New York. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and astrophysics — I use neural networks and statistical models to understand the fundamental physics of how galaxies form and evolve.

I've searched for alien technosignatures at the Berkeley SETI Research Center, built assistive technology for visually impaired users, and designed cybersecurity dashboards for a national laboratory. Whether it's a 2TB supercomputer simulation or a weekend hackathon, I love tackling problems that feel just a little too hard.
